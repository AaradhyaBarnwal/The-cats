The Cats

The Cats have been a very loyal ,playful , companion to us. Recently they have gained quite the popularity these days.So,why not acknowledge them a bit,why not know a bit more about them.

Indian short hair

Indian short hair also known as Desi cat and Indian Billi. Despite its highly adaptable , resilient,intelligent and low-maintanace people of India often prefer foreign breeds which results in various negative consequences . These cats often ear a mix of meat,curd,plain rice ,eggs etc.

British Short Hair

The british short hair is the pedigree version of the traditional British domestic cat, with a distively stocky body,thick coat, and broad face. The most familiar colour variant is the "British Blue", with a solid grey-blue coat, copper-coloured eyes, and a medium-sized tail.

Siberian cat

The siberian is a centuries-old landrace of domestic cat in Russia, and recently developed as a formal breed with standards promulgated the world over since the late-1980s. Since 2006 the breed is recognised for registry and championship status with all major cat registries.

Ragdoll

The ragdoll is a breedof a cat with a distinct colorpoint coat and blue eyes. Its morphology is large and weighty, and it has a semu-long and silky soft coat. American breeder Ann Baker developed Ragdolls in the 1960's. They are best known for their docile,placid temperature and affectionate nature.

Burmilla

The Burmilla is a breed of domestic cat, that originated in the United Kingdom in 1981 . It is a cross between the Chinchilla Persian and Burmese cats. In certain cat registries the breed falls under the Asian group, and is sometimes referred to as the Asian Shaded.

Turkish Van

The Turkish Van is a semi-long-haired, standardised breed of domestic cat, which was developed in the United Kingdom from a selection of cats obtained from various cities of mordern Turkey, especially southeast Turkey.

Facts

The average age for an indoor cat is 15 years while the average age for an outdoor cat is only 3-5 years.

A cat's nose print is unique, like a human fingerprint.

They have an extra organ called the Jacobson's organ, which helps them "taste" scents in the air.

Cats have a third eyelid called a "nicitating membrane" for eye protection.

Cats can make over 100 different vocal sounds (dogs make about 10)

A cat's brain is 90% similar to a human's structure.

Each cat has a unique meow-many develop special ones just for their humans.

Cats rarely meow at other cats; they mostly meow to communicate with people.

They use body language more than sound to communicate.

They can learn tricks and commands-it just has to be worth it for them.

A cat rubbing against you means they're marking you as theirs.

Cats get hiccups, just like humans.
